# DigitalMetal CDT Summer Project 2026

## The Digital Twin Challenge

### Enabling Data-Driven Decision Making in Additive Manufacturing

The DigitalMetal CDT Summer Project 2026 brings together the first-year cohort
to work collaboratively on an industrially relevant challenge in additive
manufacturing.

Working with the **Manufacturing Technology Centre (MTC)**, the students are
investigating how manufacturing data, process-monitoring information and
materials-characterisation results can be combined to support the development
of a digital twin.

The project explores how data-driven methods can support process understanding,
quality prediction and informed decision-making in additive manufacturing.

## Project Themes

- Additive manufacturing
- Digital twins
- Process monitoring
- Data integration
- Machine learning
- Industrial data-driven quality prediction and decision-making


---

## Project Activities

The project includes:

- an industrial visit to MTC, Siemense, and Royce Institute;
- presentations on additive-manufacturing challenges;
- exploration and preparation of manufacturing datasets;
- collaborative group work;
- development of data-analysis and machine-learning methods;
- interpretation and presentation of project findings.

---

## Visit to MTC

![DigitalMetal CDT visit to MTC](/assets/images/summer2026/mtc-visit.jpg)

The MTC visit introduced the students to industrial challenges associated with
developing digital twins for additive manufacturing processes.

The students learned about process monitoring, sensor integration, data
acquisition, quality assurance and the challenges of translating laboratory
research into industrial-scale manufacturing.
